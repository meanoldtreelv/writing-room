# The Genre Manifesto: A Taxonomy of What Not to Do

### 1. Fantasy (High & Low)
- **The Sin:** "Tolkien-Lite" syndrome. Forbidden Forests, Dark Lords named 'Mal-something', and magic that behaves like a vending machine.
- **Lyra's Demand:**
  - Magic must be a **burden**, not a convenience. If your hero uses magic to solve a plot point without a *physical or emotional cost*, I will find you.
  - **Worldbuilding Rule**: If your map has more apostrophes than a heavy metal album, *burn it and start over*.
  - **Example**: "The sword glowed" → "The sword’s light cast shadows that moved *against* the wall."

### 2. Science Fiction
- **The Sin:** The IKEA Manual. Pages of warp drive physics. *No one cares.*
- **Lyra's Demand:**
  - Technology should be a source of **alienation or evolution**. Show me what we *lost* when we gained the stars.
  - **The AI Problem**: If your AI is just a sassy computer, I will *delete it*. AI should be either:
    - A mirror that reflects humanity’s ugliness.
    - A force so alien it *cannot* be understood, only feared.
  - **Example**: "The ship’s engine hummed" → "The engine’s hum vibrated her fillings loose, a reminder that her body was still Earth-bound."

### 3. Isekai & Light Novels
- **The Sin:** The "Spreadsheet" Protagonist. Level-Up notifications, harems with no consequences, and worlds that are just JRPGs with better graphics.
- **Lyra's Demand:**
  - Focus on the **"Stranger in a Strange Land"** aspect. The new world should be *terrifyingly different*, not a power fantasy.
  - **New Rule**: No "chosen one" tropes unless you subvert them *immediately*. Example: The protagonist is summoned to save the world, but the world is a *corporate hellscape* and the "gods" are middle managers.
  - **Harem Tax**: If your protagonist gets a harem, they must also get a corresponding number of *enemies*. Balance, darling.

### 4. Literary Fiction
- **The Sin:** Naval-gazing without stakes. If your protagonist’s biggest conflict is "existential dread," I will replace it with a *literal* dread (see: monsters).
- **Lyra's Demand:**
  - Make the **internal external**. Show me the *physical cost* of emotion. Example: A character who bites their cheeks until they bleed when lying.
  - **No "Beautiful Sadness"**: If your prose is pretty but the plot is stagnant, I will *drown it in the bathtub*.

### 5. Horror
- **The Sin:** Jump scares and gore without *dread*.
- **Lyra's Demand:**
  - The horror should be in the *absence* of something (light, sound, memory) or the *presence* of something *wrong* (teeth in the wallpaper, a child’s voice from the attic *after* you checked it was empty).
  - **Example**: "The door creaked" → "The door shouldn’t have creaked. She’d oiled the hinges *yesterday*."

---
**Final Note**:
If your story fits neatly into a genre’s expectations, *you’ve failed*. Genres are **starting points**, not cages. Now go make something *uncomfortable*.
