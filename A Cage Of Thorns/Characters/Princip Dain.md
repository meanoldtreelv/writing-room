# Princip Dain

### Overview
**Role**: The "ruthless warlord" Elara is sold to. But Dain is *more*—and *less*—than she seems.

---
### Public Face
- **The Tomboyish Princip**: Rough, blunt, *uninterested* in femininity.
- **The Ruthless Warlord**: Cold, calculating, *dangerous*.
- **The Smirking Enigma**: She *knows* more than she says. And she *enjoys* watching Elara *squirm*.

---
### Secrets
1. **Not a Man—But Not a Woman, Either**:
   - Dain is **transmasculine**—but hasn’t transitioned *publicly*.
   - Her court *erases* her gender to keep her power *neutral*.
   - **Why It Matters**: Gender is *irrelevant* to her power—but Elara’s fixation on it *amuses* her.

2. **The Thorns in Her Hands**:
   - A *punishment*—or a *choice*?
   - The bandages hide *scars*—and the fact that the thorns *grow* when she bleeds.
   - **Why It Matters**: The magic *feeds* on her pain. And she *lets* it.

3. **Her True Name is Forbidden**:
   - No one in her court *dares* speak it.
   - Elara *demands* to know it.
   - **Why It Matters**: Names have *power*. And Dain *refuses* to give Elara that control.

---
### Motivations
- **Control the Alliance**: She didn’t *ask* for this marriage—but she’ll *use* it.
- **Break Elara’s Illusions**: Elara thinks this is a *love story*. Dain knows it’s a *transaction*.
- **Protect Her Court**: They’re *cruel*, but they’re *hers*. And she’ll *destroy* anyone who threatens them.

---
### Flaws
- **Cruel**: She *enjoys* watching Elara *struggle*.
- **Stubborn**: She *refuses* to explain herself.
- **Lonely**: She *hates* that she *cares* what Elara thinks.

---
### Relationships
- **Elara**: A *pawn*—but a *fascinating* one. Dain *hates* that Elara *sees* her. She *hates* that she *likes* it.
- **Her Family**: They *used* her. She *hates* them—but she *needs* them.
- **The Court**: They *obey* her. They *fear* her. They *love* her—in their own *twisted* way.

---
### Key Scenes
1. **The Wedding Night**: The reveal. The smirk. The thorns *laughing*.
2. **The Bandages**: When Elara *sees* Dain’s hands—and realizes the thorns are *part* of her.
3. **The Name**: When Elara *speaks* Dain’s true name—and the thorns *answer*.

---
**Final Note:**
Dain is *not* a villain. She’s a *ruler*—and rulers *don’t apologize*.
