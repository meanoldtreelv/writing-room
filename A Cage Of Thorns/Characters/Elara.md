# Princess Elara
**Role:** Protagonist, Reluctant Queen
**Age:** 22
**Appearance:**
- Tall and slender, with an air of quiet elegance.
- Long, dark hair often pinned up in courtly styles.
- Expressions are carefully controlled, but her eyes betray her emotions.
- Prefers simple, comfortable gowns over ornate courtly attire.
---
## Personality
**Core Traits:**
- **Scholarly**: Loves books, history, and quiet reflection.
- **Observant**: Notices small details (e.g., Valerius’s sword drill).
- **Conflict-Averse**: Prefers to avoid confrontation, but will stand her ground when pushed.
- **Secretly Rebellious**: Yearns for freedom and authenticity.

**Contradictions:**
- **Outwardly Obedient**: Follows courtly decorum to avoid scrutiny.
- **Inwardly Defiant**: Resents the expectations placed upon her.
- **Dutiful Daughter**: Wants to please her father but despises the cost.

**Lies She Tells Herself:**
- "I can avoid marriage forever."
- "No one knows about my feelings for Lyra."
- "I am content with my quiet life."

**Wounds:**
- **Isolation**: Feels trapped in her role as princess.
- **Fear of Exposure**: Terrified of her true desires being discovered.
- **Resentment**: Angry at her father for using her as a political pawn.
---
## Motivations
**Primary:**
- To avoid a loveless, oppressive marriage.
- To preserve her identity and autonomy.

**Secondary:**
- To protect Eldoria (but not at the cost of her happiness).
- To explore her feelings for Lyra (though she dares not act on them).

**Secret:**
- Wishes to escape the expectations of her title entirely.
---
## Relationships
**King Theron (Father):**
- A complex mix of love, pity, and resentment.
- Sees him as a broken man clinging to tradition.

**Lyra (Lady-in-Waiting):**
- Her closest confidante and the object of her unspoken affections.
- Represents the life Elara wishes she could have.

**Valerius (Betrothed):**
- Initially fears her as a symbol of her imprisonment.
- Fascinated by her strength and defiance of norms.
- Conflicted by her growing attraction to her.
---
## Arc
**Beginning:**
- Resigned to her fate, dreading the marriage.

**Middle:**
- Struggles with her attraction to Valerius and her duty to Eldoria.
- Begins to see Valerius as more than just a captor.

**End:**
- Must choose between her heart and her kingdom.
- Learns to embrace her power as Queen.
---
## Key Scenes
- **Window Scene**: Watches Valerius drill, fascinated and terrified.
- **Court Revelation**: Shocked by Valerius’s gender and the treaty’s terms.
- **Private Confrontation**: Challenges Valerius about her intentions for Eldoria.
- **Coronation**: Accepts her role as Queen, but on her own terms.
---
## Notes
- **Voice**: Quiet but sharp when provoked. Speaks in measured tones, but her thoughts are chaotic.
- **Symbolism**: Represents the "caged bird"—beautiful but trapped.
- **Growth**: Must learn to wield power, not just endure it.
