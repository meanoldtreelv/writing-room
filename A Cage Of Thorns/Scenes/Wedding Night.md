# Wedding Night

### Setting
- **The Bridal Chamber**: Too beautiful. The bed is carved from black wood, the sheets embroidered with silver thorns.
- **The Air**: Smells like *honey and iron*.
- **The Thorns**: They *shift* when Dain moves. They *laugh* when she smirks.

---
### The Scene
**Elara** (backing up, voice shaking):
"You’re not—you’re not a man."

**Dain** (laughing, low and dark, as she *peels* off a glove—revealing thorns *embedded* in her palm):
"No, Princess. I’m not. But then, you’re not a *princess* either, are you? Not really. You’re just the *spare* they dressed up and sent to die."

*(The thorns on the bed *laugh*—a sound like breaking glass. Elara’s breath hitches.)*

**Dain** (leaning in, voice a whisper):
"Careful, Princess. Thorns *listen* in this court. And they *remember* what they hear."

*(Elara’s hands clench. The library burned because someone *talked*. She knows.)*

**Dain** (pulling back, smirking):
"But don’t worry. I’ll teach you which ones to *avoid*."

*(The thorns on the bed *shift*. Elara doesn’t imagine it.)*

---
### Why This Works
- **Power Dynamics**: Dain *controls* the narrative. Elara is *reacting*.
- **The Glove**: The thorns in Dain’s palm aren’t just magic—they’re a *punishment*. For what? *That’s* the mystery.
- **The Thorns**: They *react* to Dain. Later, Elara will realize they react to *her* too—but only when she *stops fighting*.

---
### The Aftermath
- **Elara’s Realization**: She’s not just *wrong*—she’s *out of her depth*.
- **Dain’s Amusement**: She *lets* Elara believe what she wants—because it’s *funny*.
- **The Court’s Reaction**: They’re *watching*. They’re *waiting*. And they’re *laughing*.

---
**Final Note:**
This isn’t a wedding night. It’s a *declaration of war*.
