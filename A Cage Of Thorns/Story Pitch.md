# A Cage Of Thorns

**Premise:**
To save her kingdom from famine, Princess Elara is sold into a marriage alliance with **Princip Dain of Veyne**—a figure her family describes as a *‘devilishly beautiful man’* and a *‘ruthless warlord’*. But when Elara finally meets Dain on their wedding night, she realizes two things:
1. **Dain is not a man.**
2. **Dain’s people never use gendered language for her at all.**

The trade alliance isn’t just about politics—it’s about **magic**. The Veyne bloodline manipulates thorns, and Elara’s royal line is the only one that can *survive* them. But Dain’s true nature is a **weapon**: her court *erases* her gender to keep her power *neutral*, and Elara’s insistence on calling her *‘him’* is a **deliberate provocation**—one Dain meets with a *smirk* every time.

**The Unseelie Influence:**
Dain’s court operates on **Unseelie rules**—no lies, only omissions; gifts that are curses; and a magic that *feeds* on pain and *twists* the truth. The thorns in the castle walls *listen*, the gifts *bite*, and every kindness comes with a *barb*.

**The Thorn Magic:**
- Only works on those who *love* Dain—but love is *complicated* when your partner *refuses to be defined*.
- **Cost**: It *draws blood*. Dain’s hands are always wrapped in thorn-stitched bandages—*hiding something*.
- **Twist**: Elara’s bloodline is *immune* to the thorns. Dain doesn’t know why—yet.

**The Conflict:**
- **Elara’s Defiance**: She clings to the idea of Dain as a *man* because admitting she’s wrong feels like *losing*.
- **Dain’s Control**: She *lets* Elara be wrong—because it *amuses* her, and because her power isn’t in her gender—it’s in her *control*.
- **The Court’s Cruelty**: They don’t *lie*—they just make sure the truth *hurts* more.

**Themes:**
- **Power and Perception**: What happens when the world *refuses* to see you as you are?
- **Love as a Weapon**: Can you love someone who *won’t let you* define them?
- **The Cost of Magic**: Every gift is a curse. Every truth is a *knife*.

**Inspirations:**
- *Merry Gentry* (Unseelie Court’s cruelty and glamour).
- *The Cruel Prince* (political marriages and hidden power).
- *The Goblin Emperor* (gender, language, and courtly intrigue).

**Endgame:**
By the end, Elara and Dain must decide:
- Is their alliance *worth* the lies?
- Can love exist when *truth* is the enemy?
- And what happens when the thorns *choose* a side?
