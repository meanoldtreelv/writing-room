# David
**Role:** Protagonist, Cultural Outsider
**Age:** 32
**Appearance:**
- Tall, with the slightly disheveled look of someone who works too much.
- Dark circles under his eyes from late nights.
- Dresses in business casual (button-downs, slacks) but loosens his tie after hours.
- Light brown hair, perpetually tousled from running his hands through it.
---
## Personality
**Core Traits:**
- **Dedicated**: Throws himself into work to avoid personal emptiness.
- **Curious**: Drawn to mysteries (e.g., the café’s vague directions).
- **Naive**: Underestimates the darkness lurking beneath Tokyo’s surface.
- **Loyal**: Values trust, which may be exploited by others.

**Contradictions:**
- **Outwardly Confident**: Competent in boardrooms but lost in personal life.
- **Inwardly Adrift**: Uses work to avoid confronting his loneliness.
- **Moral Flexibility**: Willing to bend rules if it means belonging somewhere.

**Lies He Tells Himself:**
- "I’m in control of my life."
- "This café is just a reward, nothing more."
- "I can handle whatever Tokyo throws at me."

**Wounds:**
- **Isolation**: Feels disconnected from both his home culture and Japan.
- **Burnout**: His job is consuming him, leaving no room for personal fulfillment.
- **Trust Issues**: Struggles to read people’s true intentions (e.g., Kenji’s invitation).
---
## Motivations
**Primary:**
- To escape the sterility of his corporate life.
- To find a place where he belongs.

**Secondary:**
- To uncover the café’s secrets (once he realizes something is off).
- To protect the girls (if he discovers they’re in danger).

**Secret:**
- Yearns for connection, even if it’s unhealthy.
---
## Relationships
**Kenji Tanaka (Counterpart):**
- **Role**: The bridge between David’s corporate world and the café.
- **Dynamic**: Is Kenji a friend, a manipulator, or both?
- **Trust Level**: David respects him but doesn’t fully understand his motives.

**The Girls (Cafe Staff):**
- **Role**: Initially seem like innocent employees, but their true roles are unclear.
- **Dynamic**: David may feel protective or drawn to them.
- **Trust Level**: Suspicious but intrigued.

**Cafe Manager (Antagonist?):**
- **Role**: The puppet master behind the café’s operations.
- **Dynamic**: Charismatic but dangerous.
- **Trust Level**: None—this person is likely exploiting everyone.
---
## Arc
**Beginning:**
- A burned-out corporate drone seeking escape.

**Middle:**
- Discovers the café’s dark secrets and becomes entangled.
- Struggles with his role: protector, participant, or victim?

**End:**
- Must choose between saving himself or saving the girls.
- Confronts his own moral flexibility and loneliness.
---
## Key Scenes
- **First Visit**: David arrives at the café, charmed by its façade.
- **The Offer**: Kenji or the manager propositions him with a "job."
- **Descent**: David becomes more involved, justifying his actions.
- **Revelation**: Learns the true nature of the café (e.g., trafficking, cult, or psychological manipulation).
- **Climax**: Confronts the manager and decides his fate.
---
## Notes
- **Voice**: Dry humor, sarcastic internal monologue, but outwardly polite.
- **Symbolism**: Represents the "lost foreigner" in Tokyo, both literally and metaphorically.
- **Growth**: Learns to trust his instincts and confront his loneliness.
