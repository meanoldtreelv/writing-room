# Caramel (Cara)
**Role:** Hostess, "Caramel" Flavor
**Age:** 19
**Appearance:**
- Petite and delicate, with long, light brown hair.
- Fair skin and a youthful, almost doll-like face.
- Wears outfits in her favorite colors: **blue and white** (often frilly or lolita-style).
---
## Personality
**Core Traits:**
- **Proactive**: Takes initiative in her advances, eager to please.
- **Caring**: Genuinely enjoys taking care of patrons (e.g., calling them "Mister").
- **Playful**: Uses her youthful charm to create a sense of intimacy.
- **Observant**: Notices patrons’ moods and adapts her behavior.

**Contradictions:**
- **Innocent Appearance**: Looks like a "loli," but is savvy about her role.
- **Emotional Detachment**: Friendly but maintains professional boundaries.
- **Dependency**: Enjoys being needed but resents being objectified.

**Lies She Tells Herself:**
- "I’m just doing my job."
- "The patrons actually care about me."
- "I can leave anytime I want."

**Wounds:**
- **Loneliness**: Craving real connection in a transactional world.
- **Exploitation**: Fear of being discarded if she’s not "sweet" enough.
- **Identity Crisis**: Struggles with her role vs. her true self.
---
## Motivations
**Primary:**
- To feel valued and needed.
- To escape her past (unknown but implied to be traumatic).

**Secondary:**
- To protect the other hostesses (especially Mint).
- To find a patron who sees her as more than a "flavor."

**Secret:**
- Dreams of opening her own café—one where she’s in control.
---
## Relationships
**David (Protagonist):**
- **Dynamic**: Initially sees him as a typical patron but warms to his "big brother" vibe.
- **Trust Level**: Cautious but hopeful he’s different.

**Mint:**
- **Dynamic**: Protective of her (sees her as a little sister).
- **Trust Level**: High—Mint is her closest confidante.

**Coco and Custard:**
- **Dynamic**: Friendly but competitive (especially with Coco).
- **Trust Level**: Surface-level; they’re coworkers, not friends.

**Café Manager:**
- **Dynamic**: Fearful but loyal (manager controls her livelihood).
- **Trust Level**: Low—she knows she’s replaceable.
---
## Arc
**Beginning:**
- A cheerful hostess who hides her loneliness behind a "sweet" persona.

**Middle:**
- Starts to question her role after bonding with David.
- Discovers the café’s darker secrets (e.g., Buffet Door, manager’s control).

**End:**
- Must choose between staying safe in the café or risking everything to leave.
- If David becomes a "big brother," she may see him as a path to freedom.
---
## Key Scenes
- **First Meeting**: David orders "Caramel Crepe"—Cara plays the part but notices his hesitation.
- **Private Moment**: Shares her dream of a normal café with him.
- **Confrontation**: Learns the truth about the Buffet Door and panics.
- **Climax**: Decides whether to trust David or report him to the manager.
---
## Notes
- **Voice**: Sweet but sharp when provoked. Uses playful teasing to mask vulnerability.
- **Symbolism**: Represents the **façade of innocence**—sugar-coating a bitter reality.
- **Growth**: Learns to demand respect, not just give pleasure.
