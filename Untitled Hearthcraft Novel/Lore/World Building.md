# World Building

## The Atherian Empire
- **Age:** Ancient. Old enough that no one remembers what came before. The chantbound doctrine isn't a recent innovation — it's the water the empire swims in. Generations upon generations have grown up knowing nothing else.
- **Scale:** Continent-spanning. Massive enough that most citizens never see a mage cast in their lifetime.
- **Government:** Imperial bureaucracy with noble houses holding regional power. The empire is so old and so large that it's simply "the Empire" — there's no need to distinguish. "Atherian" is the formal name used in documents.
- **Capital:** Atheris, far to the east, deep in secure territory. Seat of the emperor, the courts, the treasury.
- **Attitude toward magic:** Magic = authority. Only nobles (by blood) reliably carry the gift. Commoners with the gift are uncommon but not rare — several per year at the academy. Often bastard children of nobles entered as commoners.

### The Doctrine's History
The suppression of willed casting wasn't a decree. It was **erosion.** Centuries of "the chant is the proper way" gradually sanding down every alternative until only the doctrine remained. There was no burning of books, no execution of willed casters. There was just... curriculum reform. A new textbook. A standardization mandate. And then another. And then another. Each generation, a little more rigid, a little more uniform, a little more certain that this was always how it was done.

The old texts survived because they were *old* — stored in archives nobody reads, filed under categories nobody checks. Maren found them because she was *looking.* Everyone else assumed the archives were just historical curiosities.

**The deepest layer of suppression:** Not making willed casting illegal, but making it **unthinkable.** The average citizen's experience of magic is distant, institutional, *other.* It's something that happens to you, not something you do. The idea that you could draw a circle in the dirt and heal your own blister doesn't occur to anyone. It's not forbidden. It just... doesn't exist as a concept.

## Military Structure: Mages as Artillery

### The Numbers
- The entire empire has **hundreds to low thousands** of active mages
- Spread across a continent-sized territory, this is *nothing.* Most garrisons have zero mages. A border fort might have one. A major army has a dozen.
- Mages are so rare that each one is a **strategic asset.** They're not soldiers — they're *ordnance.*

### How They Fight
- Mages operate from prepared positions behind the lines — "battery emplacements"
- They draw large, complex multi-ring circles (often pre-prepared in stone or metal plates, carried with the army)
- **Synchronized chanting:** Multiple mages can bind their circles together through coordinated incantation. Three mages chanting in unison produce an effect far greater than three individual casts. This is the empire's doctrine: coordinated magical bombardment.
- Casting takes *time.* Minutes of preparation, chanting, ring-stabilization. You can't snap off a combat spell. You build it, aim it, and release.
- Result: devastating. A coordinated mage battery can shatter a fortification, incinerate a formation, or raise a barrier that conventional forces can't breach.
- Weakness: immobility, setup time, vulnerability during casting. Mages need protection. Conventional soldiers guard them while they work.

### Why This Matters
- Westmark College trains mages as military assets. The flashy displays aren't just prestige — they're practice for coordinated bombardment. The multi-ring circles, precise chanting, ability to synchronize — this is battle training.
- **Willed casting is useless for this doctrine.** You can't synchronize intent. You can't standardize "feel the mana and want it to explode." A willed caster operating alone is tactically irrelevant to an army that fights through coordinated magic.
- This is why the military doesn't care about hearthcraft. It's not that it doesn't work — it's that it doesn't work *in their system.*
- **The crisis flips this:** When the flood hits, the mage batteries deploy to the western border as deterrence. They can blast water, but not a flood. They can purify a canteen, not a province. The confederation's aid workers — with no magic at all — do what the batteries can't.

### The Tension
- Maren's argument: "Willed casting is necessary and the empire has made itself unable to use it."
- The military's argument: "Willed casting can't be coordinated, and coordination is how we survive."
- **Both are right.** The tragedy isn't that one side is wrong — it's that the empire built a system that can only see one kind of value.

### The Northern Wilderness — The World's Edge
Beyond the Greenvale March, there is nothing the empire wants.
- **Mountain ranges** impassable for most of the year — brutal winters, deep snow
- **Old-growth forest** continues into the mountains — giant trees, twisted by wind
- **No nations.** No trade routes. No strategic value.

### The Greenvale March — Northern Frontier
The empire's northern border. Forgotten by the empire — no exports (brutal winters), no tax revenue, no garrison needed.

**Three ecological zones (north to south):**
1. **The Vale** — Giant old-growth trees (redwood-scale). Brin's home.
2. **The Threshold** — Transition zone. Trees shrink. Light changes.
3. **The Outer Wood** — Normal forest. Clearings. The merchant's territory.

### The Village — "Rootwell"
No name for itself. "Rootwell" exists only on imperial maps and in old texts. See `Lore/Village Overview.md` for full details.

### The Heartland — Breadbasket of the Empire
The vast agricultural center. Farmland stretching to the horizon. Small towns, villages, roads. This is what the empire eats.
- Separates the Greenvale March from the western provinces and the southern neighbor
- Westmark College sits at its western edge
- The capital Atheris sits far to the east
- This is where most commoners live and work — the empire's backbone

### Westmark College — The Academy
**Full name:** Westmark College
**Colloquial:** "the College" or "Westmark"
**Location:** Western edge of the empire's secure holdings, in the heartland. A fortress-campus disguised as an academic institution.

See `Lore/Westmark College.md` for full details.

### Thalmark — Lira's Father's Province
**Full name:** Thalmark (the border-mark of the Thal)
**Location:** Western province, adjacent to the river. Agricultural, prominent noble house.
**Lady Lira Thalmark** — the province's lord is her father. When the Greywash floods, Thalmark drowns. Her people are in danger.

### Edrath — Caius's House
**Full name:** House Edrath (ee-drath)
**Location:** Far eastern province. One of the oldest houses in the empire. Prestigious name, declining power, few resources.
**Lord Caius Edrath** — heir to a name that's heavier than the family can carry.

### The River — The Thal / The Greywash
**Official name:** The Thal (on imperial maps, in formal documents)
**Local name:** The Greywash (what the people who live beside it actually call it — the river runs cloudy several times per year due to upstream shifts in the riverbed)
**Location:** Western border of the empire. Divides Atherian territory from the Saltwind Confederation.

See `Lore/The Greywash.md` for full details.

### The Saltwind Confederation — Western Neighbor
A confederation of free cities with no magic. Across the river. Trade-based, engineering-focused, cooperative.
- No central authority — cities cooperate through councils and trade networks
- Flood management is world-class (they listen to the river; the empire shouts at it)
- When the Greywash floods, they send aid. The optics are devastating.

### Atheris — The Capital
**Location:** Far east, deep in secure territory. Seat of the emperor, the courts, the treasury.
- Brin may never go here. Or if she does, it's late.
- The capital is where the *power* is. Westmark College is where the *magic* is.
- The distance between them is the distance between policy and practice.

### The Southern Neighbor
**Status:** Unnamed, undefined. Another political nation to the south. Not relevant to this story.
- The heartland separates the empire from the southern border
- May be revisited in future stories

- Caius came to Westmark College because it's the only way the name still converts to influence.

- The province is named for the river: Thalmark = the border-mark of the Thal.
- When the Greywash floods, Thalmark is the hardest hit. Lira's people are drowning.

## Maren Selt's Search
Maren didn't single out Rootwell. She ran a **systematic search** across the empire's margins. Dozens of assessors sent to fringe communities. Most had nothing. Rootwell was one of many. Brin was the first found. Brin is first, not unique. The search continues.
