# The River — The Thal / The Greywash

## Names
- **Official name:** The Thal (on imperial maps, in formal documents, in the name "Thalmark")
- **Local name:** The Greywash (what the people who live beside it call it — the river runs cloudy several times per year due to upstream shifts in the riverbed)
- The empire names it from a distance. The locals name it from experience.

## Geography
- **Location:** Western border of the Atherian Empire. Divides imperial territory from the Saltwind Confederation.
- **Source:** Fed by snowmelt from the northern mountains and rainfall from the western coast
- **Character:** Wide, deep, wild. The water runs grey/cloudy several times per year when upstream shifts disturb the riverbed. Locals know the grey water means something is changing upstream.

## The Floodplain
The western provinces (Thalmark) sit on what was once a natural floodplain — the river's breathing room. The empire **forced** it into arable farmland through drainage, levees, and soil restructuring. They didn't work with the river's cycles. They shaped the land to their will. The soil is dead — no root network, no mycelium, no living connection to the world. Just dirt. Obedient. Empty.

The confederation's side of the river floods less because they *didn't* force the land. Their levees redirect, their farmland sits on natural high ground. They listened to the river. The empire shouted at it.

## Flood Pattern
- Annual spring flooding is normal — Thalmark is adapted (levees, raised granaries, flood-resistant construction)
- **The crisis flood** is NOT normal. Convergence of heavy snowfall, late melt, sustained rain pushes the Greywash beyond anything in living memory. Levees break. Farmland floods. Granaries submerged. Communities displaced.
- Damage is **agricultural.** Not a battle — a flood. Destroys crops, contaminates water, kills livestock, washes away homes.

## The Border During the Flood
- The river IS the border. When it floods, the border *disappears.* Water spreads across the imperial side; the confederation's side is relatively untouched.
- The confederation sends aid across the flooded river: boats carrying grain, medicine, engineers.
- The empire can't refuse — their people are starving. But accepting help from magic-less commoners is **humiliating.**

## What the Locals Know
The people of Thalmark have lived beside the Greywash for centuries. They know:
- When the water runs grey, something shifted upstream
- When the grey water comes in spring, the summer floods will be worse
- The levees the empire built are too rigid — they hold until they don't, and then everything breaks at once
- The confederation's flood management works better because they build *with* the river, not against it
- The old-timers say the Greywash used to be tamer, before the empire drained the floodplain. "you took her room to breathe," they say. "and now she breathes wherever she wants."
