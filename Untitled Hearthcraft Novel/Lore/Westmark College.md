# Westmark College — The Academy

## Names
- **Full name:** Westmark College
- **Colloquial:** "the College" or "Westmark"
- **Military reality:** a fortress-campus that trains mages as artillery. the name is the first lie the empire tells its mages.

## Location
Western edge of the empire's secure holdings, in the heartland. Sits between the agricultural breadbasket and the western provinces. Close enough to the border (3-5 days) to deploy mages quickly. Far enough from the frontier to feel safe.

The heartland separates the College from:
- The Greenvale March (north)
- The Greywash and the Saltwind Confederation (west)
- The southern neighbor (south)

## What It Looks Like
- Built on a rise — visible from miles away, designed to impress
- Stone, high, old. not ancient like Grandmother — *institutional* old. centuries of accumulated architecture.
- The main tower is where the most powerful mages train. Multi-ring circles carved into the stone walls. The building itself is a casting apparatus.
- Training grounds are open courtyards where students practice. The air hums on practice days.
- Dormitories: commoners in the east wing, nobles in the west. The divide is architectural.
- The library is the oldest part of the complex. This is where Maren found the pre-imperial texts.
- Great hall for assemblies, demonstrations, dean's announcements. Designed for spectacle — enchanted ceiling displays the caster's circles during demonstrations.

## What It Feels Like to Brin
- **Cold.** Not temperature — atmosphere. Stone walls, no trees, no soil, no roots. The air doesn't carry Grandmother's hum.
- **Loud.** Chanting practice echoes off the walls. The sound has nowhere to go.
- **Bright.** Enchanted lights everywhere. No shadows, no dappled green. Everything is visible, catalogued, measured.
- **Wrong.** The mana here is willing but distant. Like strangers in a crowd. She can cast, but the echo is missing.

## Formal vs Colloquial

| context | name used |
|---------|-----------|
| crown documents, military dispatches | Westmark College |
| the dean's letterhead | Westmark College |
| students talking | "the College" or "Westmark" |
| parents bragging | "the College" (with emphasis) |
| Brin, confused | "is this a school or a fortress?" |
| the military, honest | "the training facility" (when they forget to be polite) |
| the confederation, contemptuous | "the mage factory" |

## The Irony
A "college" that trains artillery. The name is the mask. The reality is a military training camp. But the name says "college," and the students call it "the College," and the parents who send their children there tell themselves it's a college.
