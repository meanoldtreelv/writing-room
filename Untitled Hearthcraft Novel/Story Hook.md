# Story Hook

## Premise
When a village girl who casts magic by drawing circles in the dirt is sent to the imperial academy — where mages summon shimmering runes in the air and channel power through precise verbal incantations — she discovers that her "primitive" magic isn't weak. It's the original. And the academy's entire system was built to replace it.

## The Fracture
The academy measures magic by spectacle: brightness, volume, precision of the chant, complexity of the circle. Brin's magic scores zeros — not because it's weak, but because the assessment tools literally cannot see it. Her circles are drawn in mud. Her materials are blood, soup, chalk. Her casting has no chant, only intent. The system doesn't register intent.

## The Hidden Truth
The shimmering runes and verbal incantations aren't the magic. They're training wheels — a safety protocol invented centuries ago to make magic teachable by rote, standardizable, *controllable.* The original mages cast with will and intent. The empire didn't ban this method. They just stopped teaching it. Let it become "folk magic." Let it become shameful.

## The Crisis
A catastrophic flood devastates the empire's western provinces — agricultural heartland destroyed, communities displaced. The empire deploys its mage batteries to the border as deterrence. But the enemy isn't an army. It's a river. And the neighbor across that river — the Saltwind Confederation, a league of free cities with no magic at all — sends aid. Boats carrying grain, medicine, engineers. The aid is real. The optics are devastating. The empire can't refuse. The confederation's "invasion" isn't military. It's *influence.* Every village they help is a village that trusts them more than the empire.

## The Question
What is magic *for?* If it can't feed people, heal wounds, or hold a roof up through winter — what is it worth?

## Tone
Quiet. Grounded. The protagonist doesn't give speeches about the value of labor. She just... does the work. The story's power is in the accumulation of small moments — a circle in the dirt, a pot of soup, a blister healed with a thumb pressed to skin.
