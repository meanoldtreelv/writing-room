# Story Pitch

## Elevator Pitch (1 sentence)
A village girl from the forgotten northern forest — where magic is drawing circles in dirt and humming your grandmother's songs — is sent to an imperial academy where mages summon shimmering multi-ring circles and channel power through precise verbal incantations, and discovers that her "primitive" single-circle casting isn't weak. It's the original. And the empire's entire magical doctrine was built to bury it.

## Comparable Titles
- **Name of the Wind** (Rothfuss): academy setting, protagonist who doesn't fit the system
- **Beware of Chicken** (casualfarmer): cultivation/labor magic, quiet protagonist in a flashy world
- **The Goblin Emperor** (Dexter): political, warm, protagonist who wins through kindness not force
- **Piranesi** (Clarke): quiet protagonist, wonder at the mundane, philosophical undercurrent

## Market Position
- Fantasy, adult (not YA despite academy setting)
- Standalone with series potential
- Core audience: readers who are tired of "chosen one" power fantasies and want magic that *means* something
- Secondary appeal: cozy fantasy adjacent, but with real stakes and political weight

## Word Count Target
~100,000 words (50 chapters × 2,000 words)

## Unique Selling Points
1. Magic system where both paths use the same foundational tool (magic circles) but diverge in philosophy — not "special power" vs "normal power" but "conversation vs command"
2. The antagonist is a *system,* not a villain — the dean genuinely believes spectacle magic protects the empire
3. Dual romantic threads (M/F and F/F) woven through the main narrative
4. The climax isn't a battle — it's a crisis zone where healing and sustaining matter more than force
