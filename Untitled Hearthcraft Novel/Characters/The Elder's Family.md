# Village Families — The Elder's Family

## Elder Torv — Village Leader
- **Age:** Late 60s
- **Role:** Elder/leader for twenty years, since his father died. The role isn't elected — it's *accepted.* Someone has to talk to the merchant, keep the village calendar, make the hard calls.
- **Gift:** None.
- **Personality:** Thin, weathered, stubborn. He's the only one with vague awareness of the empire. He's seen the crown assessor three times: once as a child (the night Maven was tested — the night of screaming), once as a young man (the assessors came back, decades later, and found nothing), and now (Brin).
- **The guilt:** He knew what Grandmother did fifty years ago. He was there. He heard it. He also knew Maven had the gift, and he never told the assessors the truth about what happened to the first party. He let the empire think the first assessors simply never reported back. When the new assessor comes and tests Brin, his face goes still. He's seen this before.
- **His role in Brin's departure:** The night after the test, he tells Brin the truth. All of it. Maven. The assessors. The screaming. What Grandmother did. "Grandmother protected her. But if it happens again, the empire notices. And soldiers burn forests." He doesn't tell her to go. He just tells her the truth and lets her decide. She decides. He should have stopped her. He knows he should have stopped her. That's his guilt.
- **Relationship with Brin:** He treats her like a young tree — watches, doesn't interfere, worries in private.
- **Departure:** He reads the crown's letter aloud in the shared hall. His voice doesn't shake. His hands do.

## Senna — Torv's Wife
- **Age:** Mid 60s
- **Role:** Runs the shared hall — meals, meetings, the rhythm of village life. She decides what's for dinner and everyone eats it.
- **Gift:** None.
- **Personality:** Round, warm, practical.
- **Departure scene:** Organized the village's farewell for Brin. Not a feast — that's wasteful. A good meal, full stores, a circle around the hearth. She makes sure Brin leaves with everything she needs. She braids Brin's hair the morning she leaves and doesn't say anything about the grey hairs she's found.
- **Knows about the night:** She married Torv knowing what he'd seen. She's never asked for details. She doesn't need to.

**Their children:** Grown, with families of other village families. Torv and Senna live alone in the elder's house.
