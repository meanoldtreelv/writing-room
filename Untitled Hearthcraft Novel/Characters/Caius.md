# Caius (Mirror Character)

**Full name:** Lord Caius Edrath (ee-drath)
**Alt Name:** Kasten — see "Name Decision" below.

## Root Logic
**"If I am not exceptional, I am nothing."**
Caius is the investment of a declining noble house. Everything he is — his training, his education, his *name* — exists to restore his family's standing. His magic is the proof of his bloodline. If his magic isn't the best, then his blood isn't special. And if his blood isn't special, then he's just... a person. And that's not enough.

## Core Identity
- **Age:** 17-19 (same year as Brin, possibly a year ahead)
- **Origin:** House Edrath, far eastern province. One of the oldest houses in the empire. Prestigious name, declining power, few resources. The name carries weight the family can't back up. Caius came to Westmark College because it's the only way the name still converts to influence.
- **Family:** Declining house. Parents invested everything in him. Siblings? Possibly — but he's the heir, the one who has to carry the name.
- **Magic Style:** Chantbound casting. Shimmering runes in the air, precise incantation, textbook perfect. He's the best in his year. Possibly the best in several years.

## Flaw
**Performance as identity.** Caius doesn't know who he is without an audience. His magic is always *for* someone — his professors, his house, his family's legacy. He's never cast just because he wanted to. The idea of magic without an audience is genuinely alien to him.

## Arc
Movement I: Contemptuous of Brin — not cruel, confused. Her existence threatens his worldview. If common magic works, his bloodline means nothing.
Movement II: His magic starts failing under pressure. Chantbound casting buckles when the performer loses confidence. A high-stakes failure breaks him.
Movement III: Brin finds him after his breakdown. Helps him — not with magic, with a meal. He starts to see. Begins learning to *feel* what his magic does, not just *command* it.
Movement IV: Stands with Brin during the crisis. Not converted — he still values impression magic. But he's learned the chant is a tool, not a definition.
Movement V: Enrolls in the new elective. Starts learning hearthcraft. His journey isn't about abandoning who he was — it's about adding who he could be.

## Key Relationships
- **Brin:** Philosophical collision becomes respect becomes romance. He starts by being unable to categorize her and ends by being unable to imagine the world without her.
- **Lira:** Professional respect. Lira understands politics; Caius understands power. They're useful to each other but not close — the romantic tension is entirely Brin-focused.

## Signature Moments
- **The Duel (Movement II):** Brin wraps herself in a protective circle — designed for warmth, not defense. Caius blasts through it easily. But he's confused. "That wasn't a shield. What were you trying to do?" "I was cold." This is the first crack.
- **The Breakdown (Movement III):** High-stakes evaluation. Chant comes out wrong. Mana backlash. Not badly hurt, but the humiliation is devastating. He hides. Brin finds him.
- **The Soup Scene (Movement III):** After the breakdown. Brin gives him food. He eats and something breaks open. "How did you know I was hungry?" "You were shaking. People shake when they're hungry." "No. People shake when they're afraid." "...Eat your soup."

## Thematic Function
Caius is the *audience surrogate for the academy's worldview.* He genuinely believes impression magic is superior — and his arguments are compelling. He's not wrong that a nation needs deterrence. He's wrong that deterrence is the only thing magic is for. His conversion (partial, nuanced) shows that the two paths can coexist.

## Name Decision
**RESOLVED: Caius.** Full name: Lord Caius Edrath (ee-drath). The name fits the arc — he needs a name that's slightly too big for him so he can grow into it differently than expected.
