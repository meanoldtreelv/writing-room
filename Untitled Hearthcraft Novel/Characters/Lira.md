# Lira (The Bastard)

**Full name:** Lady Lira Thalmark (hidden — entered as commoner)

## Root Logic
**"I will earn a name that isn't borrowed."**
Lira exists in the tension between two worlds. She carries noble blood but lives as a commoner. She knows the system is rigged because she's seen it from both sides. Her survival strategy is *legibility* — she makes herself useful enough that nobody asks questions she can't afford to answer.

## Core Identity
- **Age:** 17-19
- **Origin:** Thalmark, western province. Her father is the Lord of Thalmark — a prominent house adjacent to the river the locals call the Greywash. She's entered as a commoner. The academy doesn't know who her father is.
- **Cover Story:** Entered as a commoner. The academy knows she has the gift; they don't know (officially) who her father is.
- **Magic Style:** Hybrid, poorly. Not enough impression for the nobles, not enough hearthcraft for authenticity. A little of both, mastered by neither. This is the source of her political mind — she compensates for magical mediocrity with social intelligence.

## Flaw
**Trust as currency.** Lira treats every relationship as a transaction because every relationship she's had *has been* one. Her noble parent kept her as a contingency. Her common mother loved her but couldn't protect her. She's learned that closeness is a vulnerability, so she manages it — carefully, strategically, never letting anyone see the full hand.

## Arc
Movement I: Assigned as Brin's roommate (commoner housing). Recognizes Brin's casting style from old texts in her noble parent's library. Starts asking careful questions.
Movement II: Reveals herself to Brin (not the reader yet — drip the truth). Begins translating the political landscape. Becomes Brin's advocate in the world of power.
Movement III: Deepens her relationship with Brin. The political ally becomes a personal one. The "cute girls doing cute things" thread starts here — study sessions, small kindnesses, stolen moments.
Movement IV: Identity exposed. Noble parent arrives, embarrassed, furious. Political fallout hits Brin by association. Lira has to choose: reclaim her noble name for political leverage, or reject it and stand with Brin as herself.
Movement V: Uses revealed identity to lobby for commoner-accessible magical education. She becomes the political engine for what Brin's philosophy can't accomplish alone.

## Key Relationships
- **Brin:** Translates hearthcraft's value into the language of power. Also develops genuine romantic feelings — which terrifies her, because feelings are a vulnerability she can't afford. The "cute girls doing cute things" dynamic is her learning to let her guard down.
- **Caius:** Professional respect. They understand each other's worlds — Caius the noble world, Lira the political world. Useful allies, not friends.
- **Maren Selt:** The professor may know or suspect Lira's identity. Their relationship is careful — mutual recognition of being people who operate in shadows.

## Signature Moments
- **The Question (Movement I):** "Where did you learn to draw circles like that?" Brin: "My grandmother." Lira: "Smart woman." [Lira is already connecting dots.]
- **The Reveal to Brin (Movement II):** Not dramatic. A quiet conversation. "My father is Lord Thalmark. I'm here as a commoner. I need you to not treat me differently." Brin: "I don't treat anyone differently." Lira: "...That's what I'm afraid of."
- **The Exposure (Movement IV):** Noble parent arrives. Public scene. Lira's face when she's outed — not shame, not fear. *Rage.* Cold, controlled, political rage.
- **The Choice (Movement V):** She could leverage her name for personal power. Instead, she uses it to open doors for others. This is the first time she does something that isn't strategic — it's *right.*

## Thematic Function
Lira is the *political translator.* Brin can prove hearthcraft works. Caius can demonstrate that impression magic has limits. But Lira is the one who can make the system *change* — because she speaks its language. She's also the living proof that the empire's class system is a lie: her noble blood gives her no more magical ability than any commoner.

## Name Notes
- "Lira" — short, punchy, musical and monetary (coin/currency). Her name is both art and transaction. She was a deal.
- Full name: **Lady Lira Thalmark.** Her father is Lord of Thalmark, the western province named for the river. When the Greywash floods, her people drown.

## The Romantic Tangle
Lira and Brin's relationship should develop alongside (not in competition with) Brin/Caius. The tangle isn't jealousy — it's Brin discovering that connection comes in different shapes, and neither invalidates the other. Lira offers political understanding and shared vulnerability. Caius offers philosophical challenge and growth. Brin doesn't have to choose — and the story shouldn't force her to. But the tension should be *felt* by the reader.
