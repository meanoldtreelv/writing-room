# Village Families — The Storykeeper

## Ailsa — Keeper of Stories and Songs
- **Age:** 70s
- **Role:** Carries the village's *memory.* Every story, every song, every name of every person who ever lived here. She's the one who taught the children (including Brin) the village's songs — not the magical ones, the *other* ones. Lullabies, work songs, festival songs.
- **Gift:** None.
- **Personality:** Tiny, fierce, immovable.
- **History with Maven:** They were friends for sixty years. When Maven died, Ailsa sat by the grave for three days and sang every song she knew. It took three days because she knows that many songs.
- **Relationship with Brin:** Ailsa sees Maven in Brin and it hurts and she loves it. She's the one who told Brin "your grandmother sang the way other people breathe." Brin didn't understand then. She's starting to now.
- **Departure:** Ailsa gives her nothing. She just says "remember the words." Brin nods. She will.
- **Knows about the night:** She was young too, fifty years ago. She heard it. She wrote nothing down — some memories don't belong on paper. But she carries them. And if Brin ever comes home, Ailsa will be the one to tell her what really happened.

**Her family:** Lives alone. Her children moved to other families when they married. She's the village's grandmother-in-residence.
