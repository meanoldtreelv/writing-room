# Brin's Family

## Bran — Father
- **Age:** Late 40s
- **Role:** Laborer, the village's steadiest worker
- **Gift:** None. Not afraid of it. Respects what Brin and Maven could do the way he respects a good axe — a tool he can't use but he's glad someone can.
- **Personality:** Strong, quiet, hands like leather. Shows up first, leaves last, never complains. Not stoicism — identity. He knows who he is and he's fine with it.
- **Knows practical knowledge:** Taught Brin to check the garden at dawn, read the soil, know which mushrooms are safe. His knowledge is practical, not magical. She uses both without thinking.
- **Relationship with Brin:** He doesn't understand her magic but he understands *her.*
- **Departure:** The night of the discussion, he says "we'll find another way." There isn't one. He knows it. The morning she leaves, he carries her bag to the road and sets it down and says "you write when you can." The most he's ever said about love. He doesn't cry until she's past the threshold.

## Della — Mother
- **Age:** Mid 40s
- **Role:** Social center, the one who notices
- **Gift:** None, but she *pays attention.* She knew Brin had the gift before anyone else — noticed the garden growing faster around Brin's sitting spot, the fire burning steadier when Brin hummed.
- **Personality:** Sharp, warm. The village's listener. People come to Della when they need to talk and don't want advice. She hears them out, nods, sends them home with soup.
- **Relationship with Brin:** Fierce, quiet, terrified. She knows what the empire does with gifted commoners. She heard the screaming fifty years ago. She doesn't sleep the night of the discussion.
- **Her gift to Brin:** A small carved wooden circle — Maven's. Not magical. Just the right shape and the right weight. When Brin holds it she can almost hear the song.
- **Departure:** She begs Brin, quietly, after dark. "Please. Stay. Let us worry about the rest." Brin: "I can't." Della knows what that means: her daughter is doing the thing she was raised to do. Fixing what's broken. Even if what's broken is her own life. The morning of, Della braids Brin's hair and doesn't say anything about the grey hairs she's found.
- **Knows about the night:** She was a child when the assessors came for Maven. She heard the screaming. She knows what Grandmother did. She understands why Brin chooses to go.

## Fenn — Oldest Brother
- **Age:** Early 20s
- **Role:** Hunter. The village's best.
- **Gift:** None.
- **Personality:** Broad, capable, protective. Knows the Outer Wood like his own house. Goes further than anyone because he's not afraid of the dark — "Grandmother watches out for fools."
- **Taught Brin:** To track, move quietly, read the forest floor. She's better at it than he is and they both know it.
- **Calls her:** "little root." She calls him "stump." Neither remembers why.
- **Departure:** He doesn't come to the road. He's hunting. When he comes back and she's gone, he goes to the garden and circles the beds himself, badly, with a stick. The circles don't hold. He throws the stick into the trees. He should have been the one protecting her. Instead she's protecting them. He doesn't forgive himself — not for her leaving, but for not finding another way.

## Cael — Second Brother
- **Age:** Late teens
- **Role:** Restless youth, the one most likely to leave
- **Gift:** None, but he's the one who asks *why.* Why does the garden grow? Why does Grandmother open paths? Why does the merchant come only once a year?
- **Personality:** Curious, sharp-tongued, hungry for something he can't name. Not unhappy — just *hungry.*
- **Relationship with Brin:** They argue constantly and like each other completely. He's the only one who asks her about the magic — not how it works, but how it *feels.* "when you hum, what do you hear?" she says "nothing." he doesn't believe her.
- **Departure:** He's angry. Not at her — at the crown, the empire, the road. He watches her go and thinks: *that should be me.* Not because he wants the academy. Because he wants the *choice.* **He's the one who figures it out.** The night of the discussion, he says: "if you stay, Grandmother does what she did before. and then they come back with fire." Brin doesn't answer. He's right. He's always right about the things that matter.
- **Sequel potential:** Cael might be the next one with the gift. Or he might leave anyway. His story is about choosing to go where Brin's was about being taken.

## Maven — Grandmother (Deceased)
- **Died:** Three years before the story begins
- **Role:** The gifted one. The village's quiet heart.
- **Gift:** Full willed casting. Taught Brin the shapes, the songs, the way to listen to the soil. Never called it magic. Called it "tending."
- **Personality:** Not the elder — the *listener.* People came to Maven when they needed healing, not just of wounds but of worry. She'd hum, and the hum would settle things.
- **The songs:** She taught Brin the songs her grandmother taught her, and so on, back and back and back. An unbroken line of women who listened.
- **What happened when the assessors came:** Fifty years ago, Maven was young — a teenager, about Brin's age. The assessors tested her. She passed. She refused to go. Grandmother dealt with those who would take her. **It was a night of screaming.** Torv was there. He was young. He never forgot.
- **Why she stayed:** Because Grandmother protected her. Because the village needed her. Because the songs are a conversation, and you don't walk away from a conversation with something that old and that alive.
- **Legacy:** Brin carries her songs. The village carries her absence. Grandmother carries her voice — the songs are still in the root network, humming below the soil.
