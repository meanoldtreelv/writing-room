# Untitled Hearthcraft Novel: Vault Guide

### Overview
A fantasy novel (~100k words) about the quiet magic of labor versus the spectacle of power. When a village girl is sent to an imperial academy where magic is measured by how brightly it shines, she must prove that the most important magic is the kind nobody notices.

### Key Themes
1. **Labor vs Spectacle**: Magic measured by what it feeds, not what it impresses.
2. **Standardization as Control**: The chant isn't the magic — it's the cage that makes magic legible to authority. The rings aren't safety — they're surrender.
3. **Usefulness as Worth**: Brin's flaw — if she can't help, she's nothing.
4. **The Old Ways vs The Doctrine**: Hearthcraft isn't primitive. It's original. The empire didn't ban it — they made it unthinkable.
5. **Artillery vs Hearthfire**: The empire needs its magical batteries. It also needs healers, farmers, and people who can sustain life. It forgot to need both.

### Author's Voice
See `AUTHORS VOICE.md` for the full style guide. Summary:
- **Jordan's accumulation** + **Pratchett's precision.** Build through detail, cut to the heart.
- First person (Brin), close third for other POVs.
- Show don't tell. Never name emotions. Never explain magic.
- Every description serves double duty (world-building + character + plot).
- Dialogue reveals worldview. What characters DON'T say matters more.
- Humor from characters being themselves in systems that don't account for them.
- Pacing: long, long, long, SHORT. Build, build, build, cut.

### Writing Tips
- Every scene should touch the central fracture: utility vs spectacle.
- Brin's magic should feel *invisible* — no light show, no sound, just things getting better.
- The academy's magic should feel *wasteful* — beautiful but empty.
- The romance threads (Brin/Caius, Brin/Lira) should breathe. "Cute girls doing cute things" is valid pacing.
- Don't rush the middle. Movement III (Deepening) is where the book lives or dies.
