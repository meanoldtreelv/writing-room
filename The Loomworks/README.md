# The Loomworks
**Genre:** Sci-Fi / Man-Out-of-Time
**Setting:** Earth, 2126 (100 years in the future)
---
## Core Premise
Emily, a woman from the **present day**, is **thrown 100 years into the future** and must **adapt to survive** in a world that’s **alien to her**. Along the way, she crosses paths with **Callum**, a fiery Englishman with a **penchant for brawling** and a **protective streak**. Together, they navigate **technological marvels, cultural shifts, and personal conflicts** in a future that’s both **wondrous and dangerous**.

**Themes:**
- **Adaptation vs. Identity**: Can Emily **retain her sense of self** in a world that sees her as a relic?
- **Culture Shock**: **Satirical and dramatic** explorations of future society (e.g., AI, corporate rule, social norms).
- **Conflict**: **Physical (Callum’s fights) vs. Ideological (Emily’s clashes with futuristic values)**.
- **The Loomworks**: Is the title a **metaphor for fate** (threads of time) or a **literal time-weaving technology**?
---

## Characters
### Emily
- **Role**: **Protagonist, Fish-Out-of-Water**
- **Traits**: **Resourceful, curious, but overwhelmed** by the future.
- **Conflict**: **Struggles with identity**—is she a **relic, a weapon, or a pioneer**?
- **Growth**: Learns to **navigate 2126**, but **at what cost**?

### Callum
- **Role**: **Ally, Protector, Wildcard**
- **Traits**: **Hot-headed, loyal, and unafraid of violence**. "An angry Englishman has nothing on a full-grown grizzly bear."
- **Conflict**: **Clashes with future norms**—does he **adapt or rebel**?
