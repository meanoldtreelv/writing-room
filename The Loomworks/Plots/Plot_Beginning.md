# The Loomworks - Initial Plot Outline

**Phase 1: The Displacement and The Encounter**

*   **Scene 1: Emily's Awakening in 2012 (Park)**
    *   Emily wakes up disoriented, shivering, and soaked in a meticulously manicured park in Leeds.
    *   She experiences profound culture shock: unfamiliar greenery, sleek riverbanks, modern clothing of passersby.
    *   Internal monologue reveals her struggle to reconcile this new reality with her 1862 factory life, her regret over lost connections (Mama, Tom), and her fear.

*   **Scene 2: Callum's Observation and Intervention**
    *   Callum, returning from a birthday call with his sister Rosin (Irish, supporting his tailoring venture), notices Emily in the park.
    *   He observes her distress, drenched state, and anachronistic appearance. His protective instincts are triggered, comparing her vulnerability to his sister's.
    *   He approaches cautiously, offering help.

*   **Scene 3: The Spark of Trust and Need**
    *   Emily is wary and defensive ("I'll fight you off," "People don't help for nothing").
    *   Callum reassures her, offers his jacket, and suggests his nearby flat for warmth and safety. He mentions his Canadian background and tailoring shop in Leeds.
    *   Emily, desperate and with nowhere else to go, hesitantly accepts.

*   **Scene 4: The Walk and Misunderstandings**
    *   As they walk, Emily describes her (misunderstood) situation: "from a village," working at "the Loomworks factory," unaware it's now a museum.
    *   Callum, while kind, begins to suspect delusion or homelessness, finding her attire and knowledge of the "factory" odd.
    *   Subtle details hint at the era difference: Emily's reaction to cars, lights, Callum's clothes vs. his tailoring craft.

*   **Scene 5: Arrival at Callum's Flat**
    *   Callum brings Emily to his flat. The space is contemporary, clean, and smells of fresh paint and wood.
    *   Focus on Emily's overwhelming sensory input and growing realization of the temporal gulf.

**Phase 2: Adaptation and Investigation (To be detailed later)**

*   Emily begins to grasp the reality of the time difference.
*   Callum tries to help her find practical solutions while investigating the source of her distress.
*   Hints of the "Loomworks" mechanism start to surface.
