# Callum

### Overview
**Role**: The *tailor of lost things*—a healer who *hears* the stories in cloth and *collects* what doesn’t belong. His shop is half tailor, half *museum of curiosities*, and all *haven*.

---
### Public Face
- **The Kind Tailor**: Soft-spoken, precise, *patient*.
- **The Collector**: His shop is full of *objects that shouldn’t exist*—but do.
- **The Survivor**: He *carries* his scars quietly (both physical and emotional).

---
### Secrets
1. **He Hears the Cloth**:
   - A *wedding dress* sings with joy.
   - A *soldier’s uniform* whispers in pain.
   - His *father’s last shirt* screams—so he keeps it *locked* away.
   - **Why It Matters**: He *mends* more than fabric—he mends *stories*.

2. **His Shop’s Magic**:
   - The *curiosities* are alive:
     - A *pocket watch* ticks backward (it’s *lonely*).
     - A *teacup* refills itself (it *likes* company).
     - A *spool of thread* glows when Emily touches it (it *remembers* her).
   - **Rule**: "Everything here has a *story*. Some just need a *stitch* to remember it."

3. **The Plane Crash**:
   - He was the *sole survivor*.
   - The fog wasn’t *natural*—it was *threads* of something *older*.
   - **Why It Matters**: He *recognizes* the signs in Emily because he’s *lived* them.

---
### Motivations
- **Heal the Lost**: He *collects* broken things because he was *broken* once.
- **Protect His Shop**: It’s not just a *business*—it’s a *ward*.
- **Learn to Let Go**: His father’s shirt *screams*. He needs to *stitch* it—or *release* it.

---
### Flaws
- **Overprotective**: He *hoards* lost things—even when they need to *move on*.
- **Stubborn**: He *refuses* to admit he’s lonely.
- **Guilty**: He thinks his father’s death was his *fault*.

---
### Relationships
- **Emily**: The first *person* he’s *collected* who might *stay*.
  - He *sees* her thread is *frayed*—but not broken.
  - He *fears* she’ll *leave* like everything else.
- **The Loomworks**: It’s his *rival*—but also his *ally*.
  - The looms *respect* him because he *understands* threads.
  - The workers *trust* him to *keep* their stories safe.
- **The Spider Worker**: She *knows* his secret.
  - She *knits* him a *scarf* every winter (it *never unravels*).
  - She *tells* him: "You *hold* too much. Even for a tailor."

---
### Key Scenes
1. **The First Meeting**: When he *sees* Emily’s dress is from 1862.
2. **The Glowing Spool**: When the spool *chooses* her—and he *understands* why.
3. **The Father’s Shirt**: When Emily *hears* it scream—and helps him *stitch* it quiet.
4. **The Final Weave**: When they combine their threads to make a *door* to her future.

---
### His Magic (The Rules)
- **Hearing the Cloth**:
  - *Happy* cloth *sings*.
  - *Sad* cloth *whispers*.
  - *Angry* cloth *screams*.
- **The Scissors**:
  - They *only cut* what’s *ready* to let go.
  - **Example**: He tries to cut his father’s shirt—the scissors *refuse*.
- **The Shop’s Growth**:
  - The more he *helps*, the bigger the shop gets.
  - **Example**: A new *door* appears when someone *needs* it.

---
**Final Note**:
*"Callum doesn’t* **fix** *things. He* **listens** *to them—and sometimes, that’s enough."*
