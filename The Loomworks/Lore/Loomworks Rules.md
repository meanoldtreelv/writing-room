# The Loomworks: Rules and Lore

### Overview
The Loomworks isn’t just a factory—it’s a *living* story, woven from time, memory, and *hungry* looms. It *chose* Emily because her thread was *frayed*, not broken. And it *respects* Callum because he *understands* the language of threads.

---
### The Loomworks Itself
- **A Sentient Factory**:
  - The walls *breathe* (the windows *fog* when it’s thinking).
  - The looms *hum* with old songs.
  - The threads *shift* when no one’s looking.
- **The River’s Role**:
  - It’s a *gateway*—but also a *friend*.
  - It *sings* to Emily at night.
  - It *chose* her because she was *supposed* to drown—but the looms *wanted* her story.
- **The Weave**:
  - The looms don’t weave *cloth*—they weave *stories*.
  - Every thread is a *life*. Emily’s is *glowing*—because it’s *unfinished*.

---
### The Workers
- **Ghosts of the Past**:
  - They’re *kind*—but *mischievous*.
  - They *remember* Emily. They *missed* her.
  - **Example**: A worker hands her a spool: "You left this behind. We’ve been *keeping it warm* for you."
- **The Spider Worker**:
  - The oldest weaver—a *spider* who *knits* instead of weaves.
  - She *knows* Emily’s mother.
  - She *offers* Emily a choice: *Weave* your ending, or *walk away*.
  - **Key Line**: "Some stories need *comfort*, not structure."
- **The Foreman**:
  - A ghost who *knits* instead of weaves (because some stories need *softness*).
  - He *watches* Callum with *amusement*.
  - **Key Line**: "You *hold* too much, tailor. Even for a collector."

---
### The Rules of the Loomworks
1. **No Thread is Broken**:
   - A thread *changes color* when it’s sad (e.g., *silver* for grief).
   - It *glows* when it’s happy (e.g., *gold* for joy).
2. **The Looms Choose**:
   - They *hum* with a weaver’s name when they’re *ready*.
   - They *show* memories to those who *listen*.
3. **The River’s Gift**:
   - It *sings* to those it saves.
   - It *marks* them with starlight.
4. **The Weave’s Bargain**:
   - Stay, and you *belong*.
   - Leave, and you *take your thread with you*.
   - **The Spider’s Advice**: "Some stories aren’t meant to be *finished*. They’re meant to be *lived*."

---
### The Loomworks and Time
- **A Gateway**:
  - The river is the *pathway* between times.
  - The looms are the *keepers* of stories.
- **Emily’s Thread**:
  - It was *cut* in 1862.
  - The Loomworks *caught* it—and now it’s *glowing*.
- **Callum’s Role**:
  - He’s the *bridge* between the Loomworks and the modern world.
  - His shop is a *haven* for lost threads.

---
**Final Note**:
*"The Loomworks doesn’t* **take** *stories. It* **keeps** *them—until they’re ready to be* **lived** *again."*
