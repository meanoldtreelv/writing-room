# Thread Magic: Rules and Lore

### Overview
Thread magic is the *language* of the Loomworks. It’s not just *cloth*—it’s *memory*, *emotion*, and *story* given form. Callum *hears* it. Emily *sees* it. And the Loomworks *weaves* it.

---
### The Rules of Threads
1. **Threads Have Colors**:
   - *Gold*: Joy (a child’s laughter, a wedding dress).
   - *Silver*: Sadness (a mother’s goodbye, a soldier’s uniform).
   - *Rainbow*: The Loomworks’ weave (the world’s story).
   - *Black*: A thread that’s *lost* (like Emily’s was).
   - *White*: A thread that’s *new* (like Callum’s healing).

2. **Threads Can Be Heard**:
   - *Happy* threads *sing*.
   - *Sad* threads *whisper*.
   - *Angry* threads *scream*.
   - **Callum’s Gift**: He *hears* them all—but his father’s shirt is the *loudest*.

3. **Threads Can Be Stitched**:
   - A *torn* thread is a *pause*—not an ending.
   - A *stitched* thread is a *memory* made whole.
   - **Emily’s Gift**: She *sees* where the stitches go.

---
### The Rules of Stitching
1. **Stitching Heals**:
   - A *torn* dress is *sad*—until it’s stitched with *golden thread*.
   - A *child’s toy* giggles when it’s repaired.
   - **Key Line**: "You didn’t *fix* it. You *remembered* how to make it happy."

2. **Stitching Binds**:
   - Callum and Emily’s threads *combine* to make a *door*.
   - The *final weave* is a *choice*—not an ending.

3. **Stitching Releases**:
   - Callum’s father’s shirt *screams*—until he *lets it go*.
   - The *scissors* only cut what’s *ready*.

---
### The Rules of the Curiosities
1. **They’re Alive**:
   - The *pocket watch* ticks backward (it’s *lonely*).
   - The *teacup* refills itself (it *likes* company).
   - The *spool* glows (it *remembers* Emily).

2. **They Have Needs**:
   - The watch wants to *find its owner*.
   - The teacup wants to *be held*.
   - The spool wants to *be woven*.

3. **They Grow with Kindness**:
   - The more Callum *helps*, the more *doors* appear in his shop.
   - The more Emily *weaves*, the more the Loomworks *humms*.

---
### The Rules of the Loomworks’ Weave
1. **The Weave is Story**:
   - The looms don’t weave *cloth*—they weave *lives*.
   - Emily’s thread was *cut*—but the Loomworks *caught* it.

2. **The Weave is Choice**:
   - Stay, and *belong*.
   - Leave, and *take your thread with you*.

3. **The Weave is Home**:
   - The Loomworks doesn’t *trap* stories—it *keeps* them until they’re ready.
   - **The Spider’s Wisdom**: "Some stories need *comfort*, not structure."

---
**Final Note**:
*"Thread magic isn’t about* **controlling** *stories. It’s about* **listening** *to them—and sometimes,* **letting them go**.*"
