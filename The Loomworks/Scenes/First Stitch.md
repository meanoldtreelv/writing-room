# The First Stitch

### Setting
- **Callum’s Shop**: Late evening. The *curiosities* are *restless*—the teacup refills itself, the spool glows, the pocket watch ticks backward.
- **Emily**: Still damp from the river, clutching Callum’s jacket. Her dress is *torn*—not by the river, but by *grief*.
- **Callum**: Kneeling in front of her, a *golden thread* in his hands.

---
### The Scene
**Callum** (softly):
"This isn’t a *tear*. It’s a *pause*."

*(He holds up the thread. It* **glows** *like a firefly.)*

**Emily** (whispering):
"It’s* **alive**."

**Callum** (smiling):
"Not alive. Just* **awake**. Like you."

*(He begins to stitch. The thread* **sings**—*a lullaby from 1862.)*

**Emily** (breathless):
"It* **sounds** *like my mother’s voice."

**Callum** (nodding):
"Some things* **hold onto their past**. That’s not always a bad thing."

*(The last stitch. The dress* **shivers**—*and for a moment, Emily* **sees** *her mother’s hands, stirring sugar into tea.)*

**Emily** (laughing, tears in her eyes):
"I* **made** *it happy."

**Callum** (gentle):
"No. You* **remembered** *how."

*(The dress* **settles** *around her, warm and whole. The spool* **rolls** *to her feet, glowing brighter.)*

---
### Why This Works
- **The Magic**: The thread *sings*, the dress *remembers*—it’s *alive*, not just *fixed*.
- **The Emotion**: Emily doesn’t just *repair* the dress—she *reconnects* with her past.
- **The Foreshadowing**: The spool *chooses* her—it’s not just a tool, it’s a *guide*.

---
**Final Note**:
*"The first stitch isn’t about* **fixing** *what’s broken. It’s about* **remembering** *what was whole."*
