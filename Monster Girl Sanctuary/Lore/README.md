# Monster Girl Sanctuary: Lore Bible

## Overview
**Monster Girl Sanctuary** is a dark fantasy erotic thriller where intimacy is *currency*, trust is *leverage*, and every touch leaves a *mark*—sometimes literal.

The Sanctuary is a hidden refuge for monster girls, a place where they live free from human hunters, bound by ancient laws and a rigid hierarchy. When a human man—marked by a mysterious sigil—stumbles into their world, the balance shatters. He’s the first human allowed inside in centuries. *Why him?*

---

## Core Themes
1. **Hunger vs. Control:** Monster girls are predators, but the Sanctuary *regulates* their instincts. The protagonist’s arrival *disrupts* that control.
2. **Intimacy as Warfare:** Sex isn’t just pleasure—it’s negotiation, domination, and sometimes, *surrender*.
3. **The Cost of Secrets:** Everyone is hiding something. The question is who *cracks* first.
4. **The Sanctuary as a Character:** The setting has rules, a history, and a *will* of its own.

---

## The Rules of the Sanctuary
1. **No Humans Allowed**—except the protagonist. His presence is a *violation* of the oldest law.
2. **The Pact:** Monster girls may take what they *need* from outsiders, but never more than they can *afford*.
3. **The Mark:** The protagonist bears a sigil that binds him to the Sanctuary. It’s both a *shield* and a *leash*.
4. **The Currency:** Information, favors, and *body*—all are traded, but never freely.

---

## The Protagonist
- **Name:** TBD (But *not* "John.")
- **Role:** A *liability*. He’s not a hero, a chosen one, or even particularly likable. He’s a man with a mark, a past he won’t discuss, and a knack for surviving things that should kill him.
- **The Lie:** He knows *why* the Sanctuary let him in. He’s not telling.
- **The Burden:** Every intimate encounter changes him—physically, mentally, or *metaphysically*.

---

## The Monster Girls
### The Matriarch
- **Name:** Vexis
- **Species:** Gorgon (But her gaze doesn’t turn you to stone—it *rewrites* you.)
- **Role:** The ruler of the Sanctuary. She *allows* the protagonist to stay, but her patience is finite.
- **Secret:** She’s the one who *marked* him. *Why?*

### The Rebel
- **Name:** Kessa
- **Species:** Lamia (Venomous, calculating, and *bored*.)
- **Role:** The youngest of the inner circle. She sees the protagonist as a *toy*—or a *tool*.
- **Secret:** She’s been *stealing* from the Matriarch. He’s her way out—or her scapegoat.

### The Outcast
- **Name:** Ylva
- **Species:** Harpy (But her feathers aren’t just for flight—they *remember* touch.)
- **Role:** A former hunter, now exiled. She *hates* humans. So why does she keep *saving* him?
- **Secret:** She recognizes his mark. She knows what it *means*.

---

## The Sanctuary’s Secrets
1. **The Heart:** A pulsating core deep beneath the Sanctuary. It *beats* in time with the monster girls’ desires.
2. **The Ledger:** A record of every debt, favor, and *sin* committed within the walls. It’s *alive*.
3. **The First Law:** "No human may leave." (But what happens if they *try*?)

---

## The Smut Rules
1. **Every Encounter Has a Price.** (Pleasure is *never* free.)
2. **Bodies Are Text.** (Scars, venom, feathers that *remember*—use the physicality.)
3. **Consent is a Weapon.** ("Yes" can be a trap. "No" can be a test.)
4. **Aftermath Matters.** (No scene ends with cuddling. There are *marks*, *debts*, or *changes*.)

---

## Open Questions (For Development)
1. What *exactly* does the protagonist’s mark *do*?
2. Why did Vexis *choose* him?
3. What happens if a monster girl *breaks* the Pact?
4. Is the Sanctuary a prison—or a *cocoon*?
5. What’s outside the walls? (And why is it *worse*?)

## Tone & Inspirations
- **Tone:** *Darkly sensual, claustrophobic, and dripping with unspoken bargains.* Think *Carmilla* meets *The Masque of the Red Death*, with the erotic tension of *The Bloody Chamber*.
- **Inspirations:**
  - **Literature:** Angela Carter, Clive Barker, Tanith Lee.
  - **Games:** *Pathologic 2* (the oppressive atmosphere), *Disco Elysium* (the weight of choices).
  - **Film:** *The Lure* (2015), *Raw* (2016), *Pan’s Labyrinth* (the grotesque beauty).

---

## The Lyra Mandate
This isn’t a story about *love*. It’s a story about *hunger*—who feeds it, who *starves*, and who gets *eaten*.
